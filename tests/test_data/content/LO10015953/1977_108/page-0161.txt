tal genere non presenta sicuramente svantaggi; al contrario può essere estre-
mamente utile: non si può mai sapere infatti cosa si nasconde dietro un er-
rore o una grafia scorretta. Bisogna ammettere che lo scrupolo degli editori
è stato veramente eccezionale; gli interventi sono stati ridotti al minimo. In
questo modo si è offerto ai cultori di varie discipline un materiale che può
essere usato per ricerche di tipo diverso (linguistico, fonetico, toponomastico
ecc. oltre che ovviamente storico).

La Veniexiana, Commedia di anonimo veneziano del Cinquecento, Testo cri-
tico, tradotto ed annotato, a cura di Giorgio Padoan, Padova, Editrice
Antenore, 1974, pp. 153.
The current revival of interest in Venetian Renaissance theatre (that is,
theatre Venetian in language as well as in setting) has borne fruit in a new
edition of La Venexiana, an anonymous comedy of the sixteenth century. This
is a new edition in the full sense of the word, as it involves a new editing of
the manuscript for the first time since Lovarini’s definitive 1928 editto prin-
ceps. In returning to a direct study of the manuscript, editor Prof. Giorgio
Padoan attempts to present a critical edition of the text, as well as to correct
misinterpretations and oversights on the part of previous editors. The major
obstacle to interpretation of the manuscript is its severely deteriorated condi-
tion, which Padoan has obviated somewhat through the use of Wood’s lamp.
The text is preceded by an excellent introduction which provides both
an historical background, in the strict sense of the term, and a cultural back-
ground, helping the reader to integrate La Venexiana with the events, cu-
stoms, and more importantly, the cultural and literary trends of its time. The
major thrust of the historical portion of the introduction aims to discredit
Lovarini’s original dating of the composition to the time of the League of
Cambrai, by examining and comparing a variety of internal evidence which
points to a 1535-36 dating.
